How It Works

Each race, players place objects on the track: ramps, oil slicks, boost pads. They shape the chaos for everyone. Score points by finishing races, laying your own traps, and outsmarting your rivals.

Time to go full throttle!

Race hard, overtake your rivals, and dodge every obstacle on track. Hit the boost pads, cross the finish line first and secure maximum placement points!

Time to weaponize the track!

Racing pauses, but the chaos begins. Choose a trap from a random pool and place it anywhere on the circuit. Watch your opponents crash into your traps and score extra sabotage points. Just watch out: the track doesn't care whose side you're on.

What Makes It Tick

Every race is unpredictable, but the unpredictability always comes from deliberate player choices, not pure randomness. The track transforms lap by lap, shaped by collective will.

Placement points are only part of the story. Hit your rivals with your own traps and score extra sabotage points. No one is out of the race before the last lap.

The TV is the racetrack. Your iPhone is the steering wheel, the strategy tool, and the weapon. Two screens, one shared chaos.

Our Story

Why We Built SaboTrack

We wanted to make a game that builds its own chaos, race after race: social, unpredictable, and always fair. SaboTrack was born from our love of party games and our frustration with experiences that leave players feeling powerless. Remember those toy car tracks you used to race with your friends as a kid? Turns out we just needed to add sabotage.

Apple Foundation Program

SaboTrack was developed as part of the Apple Foundation Program Advanced Course 2026, held at the University of Palermo in partnership with Apple. The program is an intensive 8-week course where selected students design and build real iOS apps using Apple technologies, earning an official Apple certification. The experience shaped every decision we made, from the asymmetric Apple TV + iPhone setup to the clean, minimal aesthetic.
